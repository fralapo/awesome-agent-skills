# Glossary of Named Frameworks, Effects, and Terms

All significant named concepts across the 12 books, alphabetically sorted. Format: **Term** — definition *(Book / Role)*.

---

**21-Type Wit Checklist** — Generative taxonomy of wit devices (Pairs, Ambiguity, Substitution, Addition, Modification, Missing Link, Homage, Trompe l'oeil, Puns/Rebuses, Taking it Literally, Expectations Confounded, Incongruity, Economy, Coincidence, Shift:Time, Shift:Scale, Shift:View, Alphabet/The Face, Transformations, Series of Twists, Bonus Wit); run at least 7 before settling on an approach. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**30%/90% Rule** — At 30% completion give directional notes only; at 90% nit-pick everything. Stage-matched feedback prevents killing work before it can reveal itself. *(Making of a Manager / Creative Leader)*

**360-Degree Feedback** — Aggregated notes from multiple sources, used when a direct pattern is not landing; run quarterly or at escalation. *(Making of a Manager / Creative Leader)*

**AMPP** — Four listening tools for drawing out silence or hostility: **A**sk → **M**irror → **P**araphrase → **P**rime. Use when someone has gone cold, shut down, or escalated. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**Anchoring / Arbitrary Coherence** — The first number or frame encountered becomes the sticky reference for all subsequent evaluation. Always lead proposals with the highest-value option. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Artdirectoritis** — Obsessing over grey bands of type, movement, and layout balance while making copy a design element. Drama belongs in what you say, not the typeface. *(Ogilvy / Copywriter)*

**Attribute vs. Benefit** — An attribute is what the manufacturer puts in; a benefit is what the consumer gets. Copy must name the benefit, not just the attribute. *(Guts / Copywriter)*

**Beginner's Mind** — Reinstating a genuinely open perceptual state before each project. See also: Clean Table. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**Big Idea Test (Ogilvy's Five Questions)** — Did it make me gasp? Do I wish I'd thought of it? Is it unique? Does it fit the strategy? Could it run for 30 years? All five must score yes. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Binary Brief** — Three sequential forced choices before generating: (1) Brand share or market growth? (2) Existing users or triallists? (3) Rational or emotional? Closes the aperture before ideation. *(Creative Mischief / Idea Generator)*

**Bisociation** — Koestler's term: all wit is built from exactly two elements colliding — one familiar, one displaced. If you only have one element, you don't have an idea yet. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Bonus Wit** — Hiding a second layer so the work functions without it but rewards those who find it. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Bottling the Underground** — Capturing subcultural energy 2–5 years before it becomes visible to the industry. Market research confirms what already happened; proximity is the competitive advantage. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Brag and Boast** — Gallup's named failure mode for vague superlatives ("world-class," "the best"). Convinces nobody; avoid. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Brand Image** — Every ad is a deposit or withdrawal from the brand's personality. Accumulated over years, not campaigns. Jack Daniel's = homespun honesty; Marlboro = cowboy freedom. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Chord vs. Melody** — A single execution must work as a chord (total resolution in one beat). A campaign works as a melody (minor explosions across time). Don't load melody wit onto a chord format. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Clean Table** — Van Noten's discipline: begin each project/season with a genuinely empty slate; forbid "what worked last time?" *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Clothesline Model** — Communication that travels 100% from sender to receiver leaves nothing for the audience to do. Wit requires a gap — the audience's completion IS the memory trace. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Compressive Power** — McCabe/Lyons craft standard: monosyllables, nouns and verbs, strip adjectives. Plain declarative sentences. Hemingway, not Faulkner. *(Guts / Copywriter)*

**Conflation (1+1=3)** — Eisenstein's editing principle applied to concepts: juxtapose disparate elements to generate meaning that exceeds either element alone. *(Creative Mischief / Idea Generator)*

**Context Reframing** — A weakness in one frame becomes strength when the surrounding question changes. Don't fight the constraint — replace the question. *(Creative Mischief / Idea Generator)*

**Contrasting** — A don't/do statement that corrects a misread of intent without withdrawing the message: "I'm not saying X — I do want Y." Deploy the moment someone goes defensive. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**CRIB** — Deadlock-breaker: **C**ommit to mutual purpose → **R**ecognize purpose behind the strategy → **I**nvent mutual purpose if still incompatible → **B**rainstorm new strategies together. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**The Cue** — The most critical craft variable in wit: a signal that a game is being played without giving it away. Too weak: the wit doesn't land. Too obvious: no reward. Space the stepping stones "just wide enough to require significant effort." *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Decoy Effect / Asymmetric Dominance** — A strategically weaker option (-A, inferior to A on one dimension) makes A look dramatically superior. Never present binary choices; always architect a third option. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Demo-itis Prevention** — Unless actively improving the work, stop consuming the unfinished draft. Repeated exposure calcifies a premature version as the real one. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**Discovery / Diversity / Disruption** — Tonchi's editorial triad for scoring decisions; every major creative call should hit at least two of the three axes. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Doer → Leader Shift** — The transition from contributing individual work to multiplying others' output. Requires giving away doing, answering questions, and controlling reputation through other people's output. *(Making of a Manager / Creative Leader)*

**Eagle Eye** — Assigning every sensory input — a cab ride, a meal, a conversation — to the active project. Total immersion until daily life becomes raw material. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**The Ecstatic** — Rubin's pre-rational quality compass: goosebumps, leaning forward, laughing in surprise. Uncontaminated by ego or social pressure. If nothing produces it, the work may not be there yet. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**End-to-End Integration** — Jobs's principle: control hardware, software, content, and retail so the experience is coherent and complexity is invisible to the end user. *(Steve Jobs / Visionary)*

**Endowment Effect** — Ownership feelings — even virtual or temporary — inflate perceived value. Free trials, configurators, and "your X" language trigger endowment before the transaction. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Fire Triangle** — Three-leg diagnostic for creative energy: **Oxygen** (environment, margin, freedom) + **Fuel** (purpose, trust, empowerment) + **Heat** (challenge, expectations, critique). When energy drops, audit which leg is weak before intervening. *(Fired Up / Creative Leader)*

**Five Tribal Stages** — Culture diagnostic from language and behavior: Stage 1 "Life sucks" → Stage 2 "My life sucks" → Stage 3 "I'm great (and you're not)" → Stage 4 "We're great (and they're not)" → Stage 5 "Life is great." Each stage requires a different upgrade lever. *(Tribal Leadership / Creative Leader)*

**The Fool's Choice** — The false binary: "I can be honest OR keep the relationship." Skilled leaders reject it and find the path that is 100% honest AND 100% respectful. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**Four Phases of Creation** — Rubin's sequence: Seed → Experimentation → Craft → Completion. Each phase requires a distinct posture. Applying Craft judgment during Seed kills material. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**HALT Check** — Before important decisions in crunch: are you or your team **H**ungry, **A**ngry, **L**onely, or **T**ired? If yes, address before proceeding. *(Fired Up / Creative Leader)*

**Hot-Cold Empathy Gap** — In a calm state, people underestimate how arousal will govern actual behavior. Design for the emotional state in which the decision happens — not the rational at-home state. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**The In Between** — The ambiguous, unresolved state where creativity lives — motion, surprise, possibility. Driving out ambiguity also drives out creativity. *(Fired Up / Creative Leader)*

**Inspiration Boxes** — Physical archive of tear sheets, objects, fabrics — uncategorised and tactile. Van Noten's Sponge Process: something noticed 20 years ago can suddenly make sense. A long-horizon archive pays compound interest. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Intention** — Rubin: "Our thoughts, feelings, processes, and unconscious beliefs have an energy hidden in the work." Make intention conscious and aligned before executing. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**Joker System (The Partners)** — Buy 30 minutes of fresh-eye input from someone on a different project; rules-based cross-pollination, not informal chat. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**The Mirror Principle** — The creative director's job is to amplify the subject's truth, not announce the director's sensibility. Ego is a liability; the subject's legacy is the job. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Mutual Purpose / Mutual Respect** — The two conditions that make safety possible in dialogue. Purpose: others believe you work toward a shared outcome. Respect: others believe you value them as people. Restore both before continuing any crucial conversation. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**Noble Cause** — The tribe's ultimate aspiration — so ambitious it is never checked off. Not a goal; a direction. Creates alignment where agreement is impossible. Choose the adversary deliberately. *(Tribal Leadership / Creative Leader)*

**Oil Change** — Quarterly maintenance ritual for Stage 4 tribes: (1) What's working? (2) What's not? (3) What can we do about it? *(Tribal Leadership / Creative Leader)*

**Option Aversion** — People pay real costs to preserve options. "Cancel anytime" is a door-open reassurance, not a risk disclosure. Frame commitments to give the appearance of control. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Positioning** — What the product does and who it is for — one clear claim held for decades. Set before a word of copy is written. Dove = dry-skin bar for women. SAAB = car for Norwegian winters. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Pool of Shared Meaning** — The collective intelligence available to a group only when all members can safely add honest facts, feelings, and opinions. Grows through STATE; shrinks through silence and hostility. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**Positively Good Doctrine** — If all competitors make excellent products, don't imply superiority — say what's good about yours more clearly, honestly, and informatively than anyone else. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Predatory Thinking** — Trott's framework: don't aim to be the best version of what exists. Study what no one else is doing. "I don't have to win. I just have to make you lose." *(Creative Mischief / Idea Generator)*

**Premature Evaluation** — Applying Craft-phase judgment during Seed or Experimentation phases; kills material before it can become itself. Primary failure mode of most creative practices. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**Price-Placebo Effect** — Higher price produces objectively better outcomes (more efficacy, stronger quality experience, even different physiological responses) when the product is physically identical. Discounting degrades the product experience in the mind. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Purpose / People / Process Diagnostic** — When a team is dysfunctional: first check goals clarity (purpose), then fit/motivation (people), then coordination (process). Fix in that order. *(Making of a Manager / Creative Leader)*

**Reality Distortion Field (RDF)** — Jobs's posture: hold an unwavering vision past the team's "no way," then supply conviction and resources so they exceed their own ceiling. Becomes a liability when deployed against yourself denying real constraints. *(Steve Jobs / Visionary)*

**Recognition/Surprise Matrix** — Four-quadrant filter: Low/Low = failure; High/Low = obvious; Low/High = baffling; High/High = hit. Both axes must score high before pursuing an idea. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Reverse Journey** — You reached the idea via A → B. The audience must navigate B → A. Every step backward must be as obvious as the corresponding step outward. Trace the path before committing. *(Smile in the Mind / Idea Generator)*

**Season of the Fire Hose** — Sustained overload that eliminates margin. Antidote: (1) add dry land (invest in non-work life); (2) build levees (explicit behavioral boundaries); (3) restore volition as the goal. *(Fired Up / Creative Leader)*

**Self-Herding** — People treat their own past behavior as evidence of quality and rationalize returning. First-use rituals and on-boarding design carry more conversion power than most loyalty programs. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**Social vs. Market Norms** — Social norms (warmth, community, mission) and market norms (price, ROI, efficiency) cannot coexist once a monetary signal is introduced. One contaminant destroys the social frame permanently. Choose your norm frame and hold it. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

**The Source** — Rubin's concept: creativity is received, not generated. The artist's job is to widen the channel — reduce ego, noise, and urgency — not manufacture ideas. *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**Sponge Process** — Van Noten: absorb inputs without immediately applying them; long latency before crystallization. "Something you noticed 20 years ago suddenly makes sense." *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Start with Heart** — Before speaking in a crucial conversation, clarify what you really want: for yourself, for the other person, and for the relationship. Hold that goal even when emotions spike. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**STATE My Path** — Five-step sequence for raising sensitive issues: **S**hare facts → **T**ell story tentatively → **A**sk for their path → **T**alk tentatively → **E**ncourage testing of your view. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**Story Appeal** — Harold Rudolph's term: inject a mystery into the visual so the reader asks "What goes on here?" then reads copy to find out. The Hathaway eyepatch is the canonical example. *(Ogilvy on Advertising / Copywriter)*

**Starter Kit** — Lyons: when briefing a creative team, the account lead or CD writes every creative thought about the assignment first — earns trust and gives the team something real to push against. *(Guts / Copywriter)*

**The Triad** — Stage 4 leadership structure: connecting two other people to each other (not just to you). Triads scale trust; dyads bottle it. "Next time you go to Starbucks, take two friends, not one." *(Tribal Leadership / Creative Leader)*

**Victim / Villain / Helpless Stories** — Three self-serving narratives that distort crucial conversations. Fix by asking "What am I pretending not to know about my own role?" Then tell the rest of the story. *(Crucial Conversations / Creative Leader)*

**"What If" Battery** — Rubin: systematically invert, extreme-ize, and transpose working assumptions. What if all the loud parts were quiet? What if I removed every adverb? *(Creative Act / Visionary)*

**World Creation** — Browne: extend the aesthetic across every touchpoint until the sensibility becomes an inhabitable environment. Anything that breaks the world gets cut. *(The Eye / Visionary)*

**Wu Wei** — Identify structural forces already working in your favor and let them run. The most elegant creative solutions involve doing less, not more — finding what's true and removing friction. *(Creative Mischief / Idea Generator)*

**Zero Price Effect** — FREE is not just a very low price — it triggers an entirely different emotional system with no perceived downside risk. FREE switches audiences into social-norm mode, not market-norm mode. The covenant it creates cascades through the whole relationship. *(Predictably Irrational / Idea Generator)*

## Evaluation-Engine Terms

Not from the 12 books. Adapted from [smixs/creative-director-skill](https://github.com/smixs/creative-director-skill) for the [Evaluator](roles/evaluator.md) and the insight layer of the [Idea Generator](roles/idea-generator.md). Alphabetical.

**Abstraction Laddering** — Choosing the rung between abstract and concrete where an idea has the most room to live. Move up for platform thinking, down for execution. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**Emotion Tiers (1/2/3)** — Tier 1 generic valence (happy/sad, ≤6), Tier 2 specific named emotion (defiant/nostalgic, 6–8), Tier 3 complex/contradictory (bittersweet pride, vulnerable defiance, 8–10). A 9+ emotional score requires Tier 3. *(smixs / Evaluator + Visionary)*

**Gap Analysis** — Diagnostic from the Score×HumanKind split: 8+/<7 "clever but doesn't matter" (add purpose); <7/8+ "matters but boring" (add craft); both <7 restart. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Grey Scale** — Originality calibration: 10 best-in-world, 9 best-in-show, 8 best-in-category, 7 original, 6 gratifying, 5 capable, 4 expected, 3 dull, 2 careless, 1 toxic. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**HMW (How Might We)** — Reframe a problem as 3 "How Might We…" questions at different altitudes (broad/medium/narrow) to open solution space without prescribing a solution. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**HumanKind Scale** — Leo Burnett "acts, not ads" calibration: 9.5+ Cannes Gold/Grand Prix, 9.0–9.4 shortlist, 8–8.9 Bronze/Silver, 7–7.9 a real act needing work, <7 do not present. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Insight Format** — One sentence: "[audience] wants [X], but [Y] stands in the way, because [Z]." Every idea ties to one. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**JTBD (Jobs To Be Done)** — What "job" does the audience hire the communication (or product) to do? Reframes need around progress sought, not demographics. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**Kill Your Darlings** — After choosing a favorite idea, argue against it. If the counter-argument is stronger than the idea, the idea is weak. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Method Rotation** — Generate with ≥3 methods from different categories (structural / association / inversion), then switch to a new method each refinement pass. One method = one blind spot. *(smixs / Idea Generator + Evaluator)*

**Multi-Perspective Panel** — Score an idea from four seats — Copywriter (craft), Idea Generator (originality), Creative Leader (consumer), Visionary (Cannes-jury) — reusing the skill's own roles as evaluation lenses. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Pollard Four Points** — Insight sequence: Problem → Insight → Advantage → Strategy. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**Pollard 7-Level Taxonomy** — business / brand / tagline / advertising / campaign / non_advertising / execution. Set the level before generating; level/brief mismatch is the #1 cause of creative-meeting friction. *(smixs / Idea Generator + Evaluator)*

**Saturation Cap** — Empirical originality limit: if 3+ known campaigns use the same mechanic, cap originality at 7; saturated pattern caps at 6 unless a structurally new variant. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Serial-Order Effect** — The first three ideas are statistically the most conventional; mark them as warmup and bias toward ideas 5–12. *(smixs / Idea Generator)*

**Simplicity as Violence** — If an idea can't be explained in one sentence, it isn't an idea — it's a plan. *(smixs / Evaluator)*

**Specificity Test** — Swap your brand for a competitor; if the idea/line still works, originality ≤5 — it belongs to the category, not the brand. *(smixs / Evaluator + Copywriter)*

**Tension Spotting** — Locate one live tension to build on: Cultural (society says vs. does), Category (promises vs. delivers), Human (wants vs. obstacle). *(smixs / Idea Generator)*
