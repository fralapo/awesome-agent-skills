# Glossary

**4 Pillars (buyer-seller relationship)** — Empathy → Trust → Value → Confidence, in strict order (Ch7)
**4Ps** — Price, Product, Place, Promotion; the controllable marketing mix (Ch1)
**4U's** — Unworkable, Unavoidable, Urgent, Underserved: framework for diagnosing a problem worth solving (Ch5)
**80/20 customer audit** — ranking customers by profit contribution to identify the small fraction generating most profit (Ch6, Ch8)
**AIDA** — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action: the advertising response model (Ch1)
**Aided vs. unaided awareness** — prompted recognition vs. spontaneous recall (Ch1, Ch2)
**BATNA** — Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement; sets your real negotiating floor (Ch7)
**BCG Matrix** — Star/Cash Cow/Question Mark/Dog portfolio classification by growth × share (Ch1)
**Benefit segmentation** — grouping customers by the specific benefit they seek from a category (Ch2)
**BLAC** — Blatant/Latent/Aspirational/Critical, the consumer-market variant of the 4U's (Ch5)
**Bootstrapping discipline** — deliberately constrained capital to force spending discipline (Ch6)
**Break-even volume** — Fixed Costs ÷ (Price − Unit Variable Cost) (Ch3)
**BLAC needs matrix** — see BLAC
**CENTER** — Core posture, Eye contact, Natural gestures, Tone/tempo/timing, Expressions, Regions/territory — nonverbal credibility framework (Ch7)
**Commoditization** — the designed process of turning a heterogeneous good into a standardized, gradeable commodity (Ch8)
**Compromise Effect** — the middle option in a choice set disproportionately wins due to relative position, not objective quality (Ch8)
**Consideration set** — the subset of the evoked set a consumer would seriously consider buying (Ch1)
**CORE** — "Capability Of Really Exceptional Value," the single reflexive reason customers give for using you (Ch5)
**CPM** — Cost per thousand; (Cost of ad × 1,000) ÷ Circulation (Ch3)
**CQ** — Cultural Quality fit, a hiring lens: additive/dilutive to capability, multiplying/dividing culture (Ch6)
**Diamond framework** — business model = customer value proposition + go-to-market model + tech/ops model + cash flow formula (Ch5)
**Diffusion of Innovation** — Rogers' adoption curve: Innovators, Early Adopters, Early Majority, Late Majority, Laggards (Ch1)
**Evoked set** — all brands that come to mind in a category, unprompted (Ch1)
**Five Competitive Forces (Porter)** — rivalry, buyer power, supplier power, threat of entrants, threat of substitutes (Ch4)
**Four Us** — see 4U's
**Freemium** — free tier funding a paid-upgrade funnel; decision hinges on cash runway and exit-scale goals (Ch3, Ch5)
**Levers vs. Multipliers** — Levers cut cost/time/resources; Multipliers grow revenue/reach around the CORE (Ch5)
**Loyalty program design** — reward the true value driver (revenue), not a proxy metric (volume) that invites gaming (Ch3)
**MAP** — Minimum Advertised Price, a manufacturer-enforced floor on advertised (not sold) price (Ch3)
**MAT** — Milestones, Assumptions, Tasks: an early-stage execution prioritization framework (Ch6)
**Matching market** — a market where price alone doesn't clear supply/demand; participants must be mutually selected (Ch8)
**MSRP** — Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price, a non-binding reference ceiling (Ch3)
**MVS** — Minimum Viable Segment: the smallest group of users sharing one identical need (Ch2, Ch5)
**P/M/O framework** — Prior/Marketer/Others: the three forces shaping a purchase decision (Ch8)
**Perceptual map** — a 2-axis graphic of brand positioning vs. competitors, built from research (Ch1, Ch2)
**Points of parity / points of difference** — table-stakes attributes vs. differentiating attributes (Ch2)
**Post-purchase dissonance** — buyer's remorse; heavy-user marketing should reassure, not re-educate (Ch1)
**Rich vs. King** — the founder trade-off between maximizing financial return vs. maximizing control (Ch6)
**Russian Doll Packaging** — free tier → limited paid → personal → workgroup → corporate edition upsell ladder (Ch5)
**Rule of Six** — team-structure thresholds: first 6 = de facto founders, next ~36 need a management layer (Ch6)
**SG&A** — Selling, General & Administrative expense; typically inverts vs. R&D spend as a company matures (Ch6)
**Stanford Shopping Center Test** — a hiring gut-check: would you rush over to greet this person in public? (Ch6)
**SWOT** — Strengths, Weaknesses (internal) / Opportunities, Threats (external) (Ch1)
**The Ungame** — term for operating a startup under radical, unmapped uncertainty (Ch6)
**Three D's** — Discontinuous, Disruptive, Defensible: framework for a breakthrough solution (Ch5)
**Ungame watchwords** — realist, clarity, humility, credibility, delivery (Ch6)
**Value proposition template** — "For [who], dissatisfied with [current state], we offer [product] providing [benefit], unlike [alternative]" (Ch5)
**White Space** — an underserved position competitors have left open (Ch2, Ch5)
**10/20/30 Rule** — 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font minimum, for pitching (Ch7)
**10X Rule** — multiply your target and required effort by 10x rather than extrapolating cautiously (Ch8)
