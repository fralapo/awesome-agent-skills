# Cheatsheet — Quick Reference

## Value Prop Sentence
"For **[who]**, dissatisfied with **[current state]**, we offer **[product]** providing **[key benefit]**, unlike **[named alternative]**."

## The Four U's (B2B problem strength)
| U | Test question |
|---|---|
| Unworkable | Would someone get fired if this isn't fixed? |
| Unavoidable | Is this structurally inescapable (tax, regulation, aging)? |
| Urgent | Is this their #1 priority right now — or #4+? |
| Underserved | Is there real white space, or 10 vendors already there? |

## BLAC (B2C need strength)
| | Blatant (felt) | Latent (hidden) |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | best target | hardest to find, biggest upside |
| **Aspirational** | fashion/identity plays | speculative |

## The Three D's (breakthrough solution)
Discontinuous (non-linear leap) + Disruptive (changes competition basis) + Defensible (protectable) — faster/better/cheaper alone is a trap.

## Pricing Strategy Selector
| Situation | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Recoup R&D, quality-signal matters, barriers to entry exist | Skimming |
| Need fast volume/adoption, capacity is ready | Penetration |
| Luxury/status category | Prestige pricing |
| B2B, partner needs a sustainable margin | Cost-plus / standard markup |
| Comparing options with different volume/margin | Compare total profit **dollars**, not margin % |

## Distribution Intensity
Intensive (convenience goods) → Selective (shopping goods, brand-sensitive) → Exclusive (prestige, negotiated exclusivity payment).

## Segmentation Viability (all four, mostly)
Large enough · Reachable · Homogeneous needs · Responds similarly to the marketing mix.

## Sales Pipeline Stages & Benchmark Conversion
Leads → Suspects (MQL) → Prospects (SQL) → Opportunities → Customers
~10% suspect→prospect · ~15% prospect→opportunity · ~33% opportunity→customer (ratios should rise left-to-right; flat = profile too broad)

## 4 Pillars Order (never skip ahead)
Empathy → Trust → Value → Confidence

## Getting to Yes — 4 Steps
Interests, not positions → Objective standards → Options for mutual gain → People separate from the problem. Know your BATNA first.

## BCG Matrix
| | High share | Low share |
|---|---|---|
| **High growth** | Star (invest) | Question Mark (invest or divest) |
| **Low growth** | Cash Cow (harvest, fund Stars) | Dog (divest) |

## Five Competitive Forces
Rivalry · Buyer power · Supplier power · Threat of new entrants · Threat of substitutes — a structural cap on industry profitability, independent of execution quality.

## Founder Readiness Filters
- "Last day of work" test — would you still choose this?
- Timing inequality — fear of too-late > fear of too-early?
- Rich vs. King — optimizing for money or control? (rarely both)
- 7–10 year horizon — are you in it for the real timeline?

## Pitch Structure (10/20/30 + narrative)
10 slides · 20 minutes · 30-pt font · open with the single most important takeaway · introduce a villain before the hero · sell the benefit not the feature · one "holy smokes" moment.

## Hiring Filters
PIII (Passion, Intelligence, Integrity, Initiative) · 3A+3+ (Aptitude, Ability, Attitude + Authenticity, self-Awareness, "People Like Us") · Stanford Shopping Center Test (would you rush to greet them?) · references from people who actually managed them, not the candidate's own list.

## R&D vs. S&M Spend Over Company Life
Founding: ~100% product/engineering → Mature: R&D ~2–10% of revenue, S&M a much larger multiple. Budget go-to-market at ~2x product-development cost from day one.
