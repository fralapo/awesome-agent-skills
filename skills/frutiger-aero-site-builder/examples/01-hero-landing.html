Introduction

Welcome to the personal site of Aria Voss — designer, indie web revivalist, and quiet enthusiast of the Vista era. This is where the work lives: glossy, optimistic, gently absurd. Sometimes commissioned for clients in eco-tech, AI wellness, or small cultural brands. Sometimes built just because the future you were promised never arrived, and someone has to keep the aesthetic alive.

You can browse recent projects, read the occasional essay or field note, sign the guestbook, or wander out through the webring. The newsletter goes out roughly twice a year. No tracking. No AI-generated imagery. Hand-written CSS, friendly cursor, soft optimism.

About

I trained in editorial design, then drifted to product UI for a software studio that asked me to "make it feel like the future." I kept ending up at the past — specifically the period from Windows Vista to the Wii U, when interfaces were glossy, optimistic, and quietly absurd. The current AI-generated design slop pushed me out into independent work. Two years later this is what I do all day.

Currently accepting commissions for: site identity refreshes, full brand systems for indie + eco-tech, illustration work in skeuomorphic 3D style, design system documentation. Not accepting: anything that ships AI-generated imagery as final output, or projects that require fluency in "generic SaaS English."
