# Glossary

**Action-forcing subject line** — an email subject that states the message's substance so the reader can triage without opening (Ch11)
**Amygdala-decision link** — the brain mechanism converting an idea into an emotion strong enough to trigger commitment; damage leaves analysis intact but destroys the ability to decide (Ch13)
**Analysis paralysis** — endlessly weighing pros/cons without committing, caused by a broken link between evaluation and emotional commitment (Ch13)
**Anchoring effect** — an arbitrary number seen before a numeric estimate skews that estimate toward it (Ch5)
**Answer reflex** — the trained habit of responding to any challenge with a direct answer or defense, rather than an open question (Ch10)
**Articulation** — complete, distinct pronunciation of every syllable without mumbling (Ch6)
**Attitude diagnosis** — reading a room as hostile / friendly / apathetic / uninformed before choosing an opening (Ch9)
**Availability heuristic** — judging frequency/importance by how easily examples come to mind, not actual base rates (Ch5)
**B-Plot** — a secondary tension thread that prevents a story from going flat during buildup (Ch3)
**Belief/mindset effect** — what you believe about a stimulus changes your measurable physiological response to it (Ch5)
**Brain drain (attention residue)** — the visible presence of a phone reduces cognitive capacity even when unused (Ch5)
**Clarity of thought** — the state where an idea can be laid out in a structured order someone else can follow; weak writing usually means weak thinking (Ch11)
**Conformity pressure** — social pressure that leads people to echo an incorrect group answer over direct evidence (Ch5)
**Confirmation bias** — seeking, believing, and remembering only information that supports an existing belief (Ch5)
**Consistency bias** — sticking to a past position purely to appear consistent, not because it's still correct (Ch5)
**Criteria-first move** — teaching an uninformed audience what to evaluate before advocating a conclusion (Ch9)
**Curiosity gap** — an unanswered question planted early that the audience must keep listening to resolve (Ch3)
**Dichotomous thinking** — collapsing a spectrum of outcomes into two extremes; overlaps with the false dilemma fallacy (Ch5)
**Direct close** — a call to action that names the requested action explicitly (Ch13)
**Discipline** — the capacity to turn intentions into measured, repeated action, verified by data not by feeling (Ch14)
**Downward inflection** — a pitch drop at a sentence's end signaling certainty (Ch6)
**Ethos** — credibility: who the speaker is and how they deliver (Ch1)
**Elevator pitch** — a complete pitch compressed to ~60 seconds that still contains all core elements (Ch12)
**Emotional contagion** — others unconsciously mirror the emotional state a speaker displays (Ch8)
**Emotional hijack** — a state where an emotional surge causes judgment to drop, making an automatic reaction worse than a chosen one (Ch1)
**Fallacy** — a reasoning error that also has the power to persuade an audience that can't detect it (Ch4)
**Halo effect** — the order traits are presented in shapes the interpretation of every trait after it (Ch5)
**Hard power** — influence gained through formal authority or force (Ch8)
**Indirect close** — a call to action that dramatizes the cost of inaction instead of naming the action (Ch13)
**Institutional Yes** — a structural bias making inaction the default unless someone actively argues against a proposal (Ch13)
**Logos** — reason: the audience must understand the benefit to act, framed from their point of view (Ch1)
**Manipulation** — getting someone to act in a way that serves only the influencer's interest (Ch1)
**Meaning bridge** — the explicit sentence connecting a story to its lesson (Ch2)
**Minimal encourager** — a short verbal signal ("tell me more") that keeps a speaker talking (Ch12)
**Negativity bias** — weighting negative information more heavily than equally-sized positive information (Ch5)
**Open question** — a question that cannot be answered yes/no and invites the other party to reveal real criteria (Ch10)
**Optimism bias** — overestimating good outcomes and underestimating risk (Ch5)
**Pathos** — emotion: the audience must feel something connected to the theme (Ch1)
**Peacock bias** — showcasing only successes while hiding failures, common on social platforms (Ch5)
**Perceived effort** — the subjective sense of task difficulty, inflatable purely by labeling a task "hard" (Ch8)
**Point X** — the single active-verb sentence completing "When I finish speaking, my audience will ___" (Ch1)
**Process goal** — a goal defined by action taken rather than outcome achieved, used to sustain motivation (Ch12)
**Projection bias** — assuming others think as we do, so agreement feels like proof of objective correctness (Ch5)
**Prospect theory / framing effect** — identical outcomes are judged differently depending on gain vs. loss framing (Ch5)
**Question substitution** — silently swapping a hard question for an easier one and answering that instead (Ch5)
**Reason + Emotion (dual-channel persuasion)** — persuasion requires engaging both rational and emotional registers simultaneously (Ch4)
**Relevance-first move** — leading with "what's in it for them" for an apathetic audience (Ch9)
**Reliving vs. reporting** — present-tense re-enactment of a moment (reliving) vs. past-tense summary (reporting) (Ch2)
**Representativeness heuristic** — vivid, coherent detail makes a specific scenario feel more probable than a general one (Ch5)
**Resonance** — vibration felt in the chest/sternum rather than the nose; the marker of a full voice (Ch6)
**Response bias** — people who would answer negatively often don't respond, skewing polls positive (Ch5)
**Situational power (Lucifer effect)** — ordinary people in a deindividuating role can escalate to cruelty disproportionate to their character (Ch5)
**Soft power** — influence gained by making others want what you want, not by imposing it (Ch8)
**Spokesman vs. human-being split** — separating institutional role from personal empathy when facing a hostile audience (Ch9)
**Stress-enhanced memory encoding** — moderate emotional arousal strengthens memory for the emotionally charged part of an experience (Ch5)
**Surface objection** — the stated complaint, which is rarely the actual decision driver (Ch10)
**Unification move** — with a friendly audience, shifting the goal from persuading to deepening connection (Ch9)
**Unit economics** — the concrete revenue made per unit/visitor/sale proving a business model works (Ch12)
**Upward inflection** — a pitch rise at a sentence's end signaling doubt or a question (Ch6)
**Vague feedback trap** — generic praise that feels safe to give but provides zero usable information (Ch11)
**Zooming in** — staying inside one physical moment of a story instead of summarizing a stretch of time (Ch2)
