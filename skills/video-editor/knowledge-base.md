# Consolidated Knowledge Base — Viral Video Editing (by area)

Deduplicated synthesis of 19 expert transcripts. Software-agnostic. Each area lists the durable principles and the techniques that serve them. Tags [fNN] omitted here (see analysis/ALL-ANALYSES.md and KNOWLEDGE.md for provenance); items below appear in ≥1 source, most cross-confirmed.

---

## A. Governing principles (apply to every edit)
1. **Retention is the lever; length follows.** No magic duration; optimal = longest you can hold attention. Never pad.
2. **Match the audience's experience** on the authenticity↔stimulation axis. Both calm and frenetic work for different audiences. Disrupting what they came for = #1 retention killer.
3. **Idea → script → edit.** The edit can't save a weak idea/script. Vet the idea first.
4. **Less is more.** Simple, well-executed beats complex. Effects must complement; remove before adding.
5. **Show, don't tell.** Visualize every important line.
6. **Edit in phases:** cut → animate/visual → sound → color/finish. Don't blend phases.
7. **One signature, not more effects.** A fixed identity > clutter (the #1 amateur mistake).
8. **Tool-neutral:** the platform ranks the video, not the editing app.

## B. Hook
- Decides whether they stay (first 3–5s, the most important seconds).
- **Multi-channel:** first frame + ambient/music + vocal delivery + on-screen text, built together.
- **Claim taxonomy (what to say):** desire · social proof · controversy · too-good-to-be-true · problem→rescue.
- **Hold mechanisms:** open loops (raise questions) + anticipation rope (withhold payoff; one bridge, don't over-tease).
- **Framing:** raw/personal beats tired listicle formulas (audiences have a "hook guard").
- **Delivery:** peak energy.
- **Visual styles:** rapid flash-frame burst + riser (montage); clean shot sequence (cinematic); strong claim + face-fast (talking-head).

## C. Retention & pacing
- Kill dead air; for talking-head tighten to *continuous* speech.
- Cut waste, not story (add-then-subtract). A pause is fine if "worth waiting for" — "higher standards, not shorter attention."
- Beat-map the music first; land cuts/text/reveals on beats; cut scene changes on dips.
- Motivated movement only: zoom = emphasize a statement, track = follow movement, micro-zoom = anti-static.
- Visual variety sustains attention but is capped by clarity ("visual mush"); shot length = how long it stays interesting.

## D. Narrative structure
- **Short-form: HPC** — Hook (open loops) → Progression (deliver journey, keep promise) → Climax (payoff at END). #1 mistake = paying off too early.
- **Long-form: 4-pillar** — match experience → visual variety → visual continuity → immersive audio.
- **Conversion: funnel** — problem→rescue hook → process B-roll (proof) → benefit → soft CTA. Proof, not the CTA, is the lever.
- **Payoff placement rule (resolves the anticipation-vs-cut-to-climax tension):** borrowed/known context → open hot; original/unknown → build then pay off late.
- Dual-narrative scripting: run a deeper secondary throughline beneath the literal content. Visualize each spoken line; space heavy beats with lighter "interrupting statements."

## E. Movement / motion
- **Premium-motion formula = overshoot + motion-blur + easing + stagger + secondary motion.** Motion blur on every moving element and eased curves are non-negotiable.
- Overshoot-and-settle is the entrance primitive; stagger entrances; delay children until parent lands; add secondary motion (arcs/shake/bumps) for naturalness.
- Focus continuity is exact: eyeline matched to same screen coords across cuts; keep moving subject centered (camera-follow).
- High-end/3D: continuous never-stopping camera journey (3 keyframes); cut at peak velocity (motion blur hides it); expression-link related motions for automatic consistency.
- Build once, duplicate, re-skin; keep a personal asset pack (required step in short-form).

## F. Text / typography
- **Three roles:** caption (key lines only) · center/emphasis (styled) · diegetic/integrated (behind subject, on a surface, inside an object, as a matte).
- **Eye-travel rule:** anchor text to the face; never far bottom, never over eyes, clear of UI.
- **Style:** brand font; no hard default shadow (subtle soft shadow OK for legibility); ≤3 words / 1-per-beat; convert auto-captions to graphics; emphasis only on the payload word.
- **Entrance:** overshoot-and-settle preset, varied.
- **Kinetic captions:** word-synced to audio; beat-synced font variation (change font per beat, constant size).
- **Reveals:** text-as-matte (video through text), text-on-path, box-open + cursor.

## G. Sound design
- ≈ half of perceived quality; most viewers watch muted (captions mandatory; sound is bonus for those who hear).
- **Motion-sound coupling:** every movement gets a matched sound; "thick air" mental model; peak-align whooshes.
- **3-step pass:** whooshes on movement → textured SFX per element → risers/hits at transitions. Isolate the pass (mute VO+music), then duck under VO.
- **Emotion toolkit:** risers (anticipation — only before a real payoff), hits (release/emphasis; reverse for tension), drones (mystery/suspense).
- Scene-matched ambient SFX for immersion.

## H. Music
- The emotional engine; choose early/pre-production and shoot to its rhythm.
- Map a mood per segment; cut on beats/dips; sync a swell to a topic shift.
- Dynamics: pause to jolt/spotlight; fade to signal an ending. Use stems for control.
- Favor loopable, clean-cut, trending-but-not-overused audio; keep a saved audio swipe-file.

## I. Transitions
- Default to a clean hard cut; fancy transitions usually look worse (esp. between similar shots).
- J/L-cuts (audio leads/trails, at phrase boundaries) smooth dialogue.
- Hide cuts: full-screen transition, or cut at peak motion.
- Motivated transitions only: overlay (additive/screen blend), shape-matte wipe, object-carry.

## J. B-roll
- B-roll = visual of the words (clearer/faster/more interesting than a face) — use a lot. Stock = lazy B-roll. Motion graphics = best for fast crystal-clear explanation of complex/boring-essential moments.
- Show-don't-tell: every important line gets a visual.
- Process B-roll = social proof in conversion funnels.
- Repurpose long-form B-roll into cinematic shorts ("video postcards").
- Match B-roll to the spoken word; place on the word it illustrates.

## K. CTA / conversion
- The CTA is not the retention lever; visible competence/proof is. Earn the ask with process B-roll, then a soft CTA.
- Conversion reels are sales assets: make the subject's value *visible*.

## L. Emotion
- Emotional investment = engagement; emotion → shares → free reach.
- Virality formula = emotion + timing + shareability.
- Sound/music manipulate emotion deliberately (engagement superpower).

## M. Visual clarity
- Eye-travel minimization: keep face + captions + key graphic on a short central path; don't scatter or overload (comprehension collapses).
- Attention-direction toolkit (guide eye to a focus point): animate the key area / darken-blur surround / hue-connotation (red=negative, yellow-green=positive) / arrows-circles-underlines / glow / scale-position.
- Protect text legibility: mask finishing FX off the text.

## N. Density
- Get to the point fast; short-form = fast info delivery.
- Cut every second that doesn't earn the next; but density ≠ chaos (avoid "mush").
- Compress action with shot sequences (equal-length clips of one action, motion-continuous).

## O. Payoff
- Place the payoff at the END for original short narratives; front-load for borrowed/known context.
- Keep the hook's promise (no bait-and-switch/anticlimax).
- Make a deliberate pause "worth waiting for."

## P. Loopability
- Seamless loops + loopable audio drive rewatches and watch-time (short-form).

## Q. Color / grade
- Grade for the look (a scroll-stopper). Early when look drives choices or one LUT spans clips; late for per-clip precision.
- Shoot-for-the-grade: light well + color in frame.
- Hue-shift duplicates for color variants.

## R. Composition / framing
- Composition retains even when slow: rule-of-thirds, subject/horizon on grid lines, foreground for depth, text in negative space, color contrast (orange/teal).
- Focal-point composition: one center per scene (object/character/text); everything supports it; decorative detail subtle and subordinate.
- Break-the-box, multi-subject composite frames, video stacking for variety.

## S. Depth
- Cast-shadow primitive (duplicate → black → blur → low opacity → behind).
- Stacking order + drop shadow; behind-subject elements (duplicate + bg-remove); blurred foreground/background for fake depth-of-field.

## T. Distribution / platform-fit
- Same edit performs differently per platform; ask what THIS platform rewards and adapt (IG: trends/audio/watch/saves/shares; YT Shorts: search/subs/niche).
- Throughput is strategic: templating/asset packs prevent burnout and enable consistency.

## U. Character / avatar animation & hand-drawn overlays (batch 2)
- **Avatar/mascot = personality + brand device** (faceless or not). Build from a stock PNG body + your circle-cropped logo/face head; keep EVERY character the **same size + same position** so it's swappable; keep a library.
- **Easing → intent mapping:** ease-IN (slow→fast) for entrances, ease-OUT (fast→slow) for exits, ease-IN-OUT for traversals.
- **Velocity graph is the foundation of "smooth":** graph height = speed; smooth/wave the curve = smooth motion. Linear = harsh.
- **Liveliness:** overshoot-and-settle for entrances; oscillating wiggle (e.g. value → +40 → −50 → +20 → original) for idle; motion blur on every move (shutter-angle dials the amount; 360 for spins).
- **Compound smooth motion:** stack several overlapping transform effects (each a simple motion) rather than one overloaded transform.
- **Reuse:** save animations as presets (anchor to in/out point so timing doesn't stretch); **subject-swap** — drop a different character into the saved animation/preset for zero-rework variants.
- **Depth/realism on flat avatars:** opposing inner-glow + inner-shadow (erase opposite corners) for fake-3D directional light; drop shadow; nested breathing/swing idle.
- **Tracking:** keyframe an element's position to follow a moving head (manual) or AI-track to attach/reframe.
- **Hand-drawn/organic animation** (a warm, scrapbook register; can convey a FEELING the footage can't): frame-by-frame **onion-skin** (draw, 25–50% opacity, new layer, repeat; start from the main object). Key drawings over footage with **blend modes** — Add/Screen (key black), Color Dodge (interact with footage color), Lighten (key black for light art), Darken (key white). **Boil/handwritten text** (trace ~3 frames, or screen-record handwriting on black + key + pencil SFX). **Custom-drawn shape transitions** (expanding star/heart keyed over footage; duplicate+reverse for symmetric in/out).
- **B-roll : A-roll ≈ 3–4 : 1** for faceless/gaming/explainer.
- **Caption typographic hierarchy (the 2026 quote-caption look):** payload word ~2–2.5× large, supporting words ~70% above/below, uniform support size; emphasis by SIZE+position not just color. Hero text gets a **long shadow with alpha-falloff** (smooth, not blocky). **Caption fade-in recipe:** transform(position)+gaussian-blur+opacity keyframes (blur 50–100→0, offset→center, 0→100% over ~15–20f), eased + smoothed graph; vary entrance direction across words. **Bottom-gradient scrim** (masked black, feather 200–300) behind captions for legibility. Transitions only at topic/music/chapter boundaries.

## Contradictions resolved (context rules)
- **Grade timing:** early (look-driven / consistent LUT / fast client work) vs late (per-clip precision). Both valid.
- **Anticipation vs cut-to-climax:** who supplies context decides — borrowed/known → open hot; original/unknown → build then pay off late.
- **Caption placement:** anchor to the face (below-chin if face fills lower frame; near-center if face sits high), never far-bottom or over eyes — same eye-travel principle, different framing.
- **Caption shadow:** avoid the hard default; a subtle soft shadow/outline for legibility is fine.
- **Micro-motion vs restraint:** subtle anti-static movement is fine; it isn't effect-stacking.

## Style families (each dials the authenticity↔stimulation axis)
- **Talking-head** — transcript cut, J-cuts, eyeline punch-ins, show-don't-tell B-roll, centered captions, whoosh/click sound.
- **Motion-graphics** — design system, premium-motion formula, staggered reveals, expression-linked/continuous-camera, motion-sound coupling, finishing FX off text. (2D-layer and 3D-spatial sub-styles.)
- **Cinematic/aesthetic** — rule-of-thirds/negative-space, repurposed B-roll, shot sequences, music-first/cut-on-dips, borders/grain, shoot-for-the-grade, sparse text.
