# Chapter 1: Indirect Selling — the 4 Elements That Sell Without Selling

## Core Idea
The single piece of content does not sell, the body of communication does. The call to action is just the last drop that overflows the glass; you have to build 4 elements in every piece of content so the audience decides on its own to write to you. Learn to sell **while staying exactly yourself**.

---

## 1. Context — Why Old Strategies No Longer Work

### What Worked Until Recently
- A Story set with a direct CTA: "DM me 'info' to receive details"
- Case-study carousels + "DM me for more info"
- The typical format: **"I'm looking for 5 professionals who want to reach X in the next month, write to me if you want to be one of them"**
- Done well, with the other pieces in place, that was enough to bring in a decent number of inquiries

### What Has Changed
- Those CTAs have been **abused by everyone**
- "DM me info" has been on so many profiles → we no longer react
- **Sophisticated and distrustful market**:
  - They have already replied to those CTAs and got burned
  - They have seen them many times and there is no reason to answer yours specifically
  - The consumer's level of sophistication has gone up
- Strategies that are tactically and theoretically perfect in courses → far less effective in reality

### The Problem
How do you stand out without:
- Entering a price war
- Making fake promises
- Becoming everything you hate in other people

**Answer**: master the art of **indirect selling**.

---

## 2. The Base Principle — The Single Piece of Content Does Not Sell

### Think About Advertising
A single creative has to drive as many actions as possible. You work on copy / visual / elements to maximize conversion on that single asset.

### Think About Organic
**The reasoning is the opposite**: the single piece of content does NOT work alone. It only works if there is other content behind it that has built coherent communication.

### The Consequences
- The body of elements and your communication = what actually sells
- The CTA + "write to me" = the **last drop** that overflows the glass
- Without the prior groundwork, no CTA works
- The job is not content structures, it is **selling indirectly through communication**
- It applies to ALL content, not one specific piece

---

## 3. The 4 Elements of Indirect Selling

### ELEMENT 1 — Direct Experience in the Content

#### What It Is (basic level)
Shift from "how to do X" to "how I DID X".

#### What It Really Is (holistic level)
Base **all your communication** on your experience. It is a modus operandi, not a technique.

#### Why It Works
One of the reasons people choose you = how capable they perceive you to be of helping them.

> Surgeon analogy: if I do not think they are sharp and competent, I won't let them operate on me. Same for you.

You have to be perceived as someone who:
- Knows what they are talking about
- Has direct experience
- Could potentially help me

#### Practical Examples
- A piece on "how to optimize X" → explain "what YOU optimized and what happened"
- Insert specific numbers from your experience
- Insert real cases of the clients you've worked with
- Explain "why you think this is useful" + "do you have examples that make it concrete?"

#### Why AI Is Not Enough
- AI can help structure, but cannot generate experience
- Best way to use AI: start from the **transcript of one of your calls or lessons**, so at least you use YOUR words
- A social media manager cannot read your mind → you immediately notice the content is not yours
- "You would notice in seconds if I were reading a script someone else wrote"

#### Operational Hack
**Document** what you are doing, discovering and living through alone with clients during your week → those materials become the content.

This automatically positions you as authoritative in the potential client's mind, **without you having to say anything**.

---

### ELEMENT 2 — The Principle of Need

#### What It Is
People do NOT buy because you say "DM me info". They buy because they **perceive a need**.

#### The Key Principle
- What sells is the **content** you build to sell, NOT the CTA
- You can paste "DM me info" onto a piece that does not create need → it does not work
- You can say ZERO at the end + they will still write if the content creates the right need

#### Real Example of Indirect Selling
You publish Stories with comments + results obtained by clients:
- No CTA
- No request to write
- No poll
- Just shared results

Typical outcome: calls booked with people who are already ready to buy and, on seeing those numbers, take the initiative themselves.

#### Psychological Dynamic
- We buy because at an emotional level we **feel** and **want** that thing
- "You need it" in the broad sense (you do not need the t-shirt, but your identity likes itself more with that t-shirt on)
- The same applies to services and content

#### Key Questions to Answer
1. What does your target want **more than anything**?
2. What are they **willing to pay for** (vs what they only find interesting)?
3. In what **situation** are they in a buying window + have the strongest need?

Critical distinction:
- **Interesting** → they consume content, save, move on
- **Paying** → they buy

#### Applied Example
- I talk about "how to structure Stories" → interesting
- I talk about "client acquisition results" → paying

In my case, people write a lot more when I talk about the second topic.

#### What NOT to Do
- Talking about your topic hoping someone responds → spraying into the crowd
- Communicating without understanding where the target is

#### The Formula
**Awareness + Need = Action**
The more you create awareness + need → the more people write, regardless of the CTA.

---

### ELEMENT 3 — Hero's Journey (Documenting the Path)

#### What It Is
Inserting your story, your values, what you believe, the real journey (heroic or not) that brought you to do what you do today.

#### What It Is NOT
"I was in the favelas, now I am in a Lamborghini" → cliché storytelling. NO.

#### Why It Works
- People buy because they feel **alignment** + **trust**
- Trust online ≠ trust offline (no time spent together)
- Long-form (YouTube) builds more trust because I see you for longer
- Short-form (IG / TikTok / LinkedIn) → you HAVE to provide elements about yourself for people to get to know you

#### Practical Tools

**1. Personal Payoffs**
Key phrases that recur across your communication, almost like mantras.
- Examples: "things don't work, you make them work" / a "freedom" value → communicated through scenes of free life (Tuesday morning coffee with no rush)
- They stick to people and make you memorable

**2. Pinned Manifesto at the Top**
A fixed piece that expresses:
- How you work
- With what approach
- Who you want as a client
- Who you do NOT want
→ Creates automatic screening

**3. Filter Content**
Content that expresses how you think / how you work → pushes the misaligned away.

**Example**: if for you support does not have to be instant (you reply within 48h by choice because "you are not saving lives"), say so. Whoever wants an instant reply walks away → they were not a fit for you.

**4. Personal Content Format**
Tell the story of your entrepreneurial journey, including the hard parts.
- Give a 360° view of WHO you are
- Trust is not built on technical competence alone
- "A good surgeon can still fail to inspire trust"

#### Questions to Apply It
- What do I carry forward in my work?
- What do I believe in?
- What is my hero's journey (real, not heroic)?
- What am I not saying that would tell you a lot about me?

---

### ELEMENT 4 — Social Proof as Garnish

#### What It Is
Constant use of testimonials, screenshots, results, case studies.

#### The Common Problem
Many have case studies, happy clients, screenshots → but they don't use them, or they use them badly. They publish a "case study" as a standalone post once in a blue moon.

#### Why It Works
The thing that **triggers** people more than anything else = **seeing that reaching their goal is possible**.

#### Psychological Mechanism
Example: you have been publishing for months with no clients. You see a screenshot "with client X in 3 months we closed €5k monthly contracts working on A B C D".

Reaction:
- "What did they do to make this work?"
- "That is exactly what I should be doing"
- → **Need** (perceived gap) + **Desire** (I want it too)

#### Anti-Pattern: Flexing
- **Flexing** = "Look how cool I am, how much I earn" → universally hated
- People do NOT care how cool you are
- They only care about: **how far they could get** + **whether YOU are the right vehicle**

#### How to Use It — "Like Garnish"
Not as the main dish (the standalone case-study post does not work). As a **recurring sprinkle, everywhere**.

**Stories** (more frequent, contextualized):
- Screenshots of feedback received
- Results with context (who the client was, where they started, where they ended up, what you did)
- NOT just "you're so great, I love you" → there have to be concrete results
- Explain how you got to that result

**Content** (description / comments):
- When you give advice → validate it with a result obtained by a client
- In the description, explain that what you said in the video worked for client X

**Frequency**: everywhere, constant.

#### The Test
"I shouldn't be able to scroll your profile for 2-3 minutes without finding evidence that everything you are telling me actually produces results."

If I do not find proof → trust is low → no purchase.

#### Types of Social Proof (best to worst)
1. **Most concrete** (numbers, situations, contextualized screenshots) ← the best
2. **In-depth case study** (in Stories with context)
3. **Plain feedback screenshot**
4. **Video testimonial** (few views, standalone, self-referential)
5. **"You're so great"** ← the worst, generic, irrelevant

## 4. Recap of the 4 Elements → What They Unlock

| Element | Unlocks |
|---------|---------|
| 1. Direct experience | Perceived authority (you know your stuff) |
| 2. Principle of need | The gap that drives action (no need = no action) |
| 3. Hero's journey | Trust + relationship + alignment |
| 4. Social proof as garnish | Certainty that what you offer produces results |

> They determine whether a person takes an action toward you, and how confidently they take it.

---

## 5. Selling Organically = Structured Work (Like Advertising)

This is not about "making content", this is about **learning to make the content that brings in clients**.

It is a different game:
- From selling with the script "I promise you the moon" or "viral mini-content with big numbers"
- To selling while staying **true to yourself**

Selling with mega promises and hype tactics → anyone can do that. But do we really want to be that thing?

You want:
- To sell ethically
- People who recognize your value
- To work with clients who do not destroy your mental health

→ You need to do this kind of work (the 4 elements).

---

## Anti-Patterns
- **"DM me 'info' to get info"** → burned out by overuse from 2022 to 2024
- **Standalone case study** → few views, self-referential, "why should I care"
- **Flexing personal results** → the client only cares about how far they can get
- **Delegating scripting to an SMM without your voice** → felt immediately
- **A CTA on content that doesn't create need** → the nudge fails without the 4 elements upstream
- **AI to generate content** → no experience, no you
- **Heroic Lambo / favelas storytelling** → a cliché that does not work
- **Pure technical competence** → trust isn't built on competence alone, you need 360°
- **Mega promises** → you burn the community and your ethics
- **Viral mini-content with big numbers** → they come, they don't buy

## Key Takeaways
1. The CTA is the last 5%, the 4 elements are the 95%
2. Documenting the real week > writing "how to do X"
3. Create need by showing specific results the target already dreams of
4. Public manifesto + payoffs = automatic filter for aligned clients
5. Social proof everywhere (Stories, comments, post descriptions), never as a single post
6. You cannot sell while staying true to yourself without structure — it is structured work like ADV
7. People do not buy information, they buy evidence they can reach the result
8. The CTA that sells is the one on content that has already created need
9. A real personal brand = no compromises, marketing with no compromises
