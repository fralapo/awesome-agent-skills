# Chapter 7: Instagram Algorithm Knowledge Base

**Scope:** tactical observations on the evolution of the IG algorithm, format best practices, content performance diagnostics, brand case studies

## Core Idea
A knowledge base on the Instagram algorithm. Documents the evolution of the dynamics, panic moments (follow/unfollow ban, hidden likes, Reels launch), shifts in pillar metrics, and the move from profile-centric to topic-centric. Contains diagnostic frameworks, hook patterns, and repost strategy.

---

## 1. Current Algorithm State — the 3 Pillar Metrics

The algorithm decides what to push on **3 metrics**, no longer likes / followers:

### Saves
- Very heavy weight, **direct impact on reach**
- Signal: "this profile publishes content I'm interested in"
- More reliable than a follow for the algorithm (the user is more "intentional")
- **Trigger**: useful, saveable content, frameworks, lists, cheatsheets

### Watch Time
- Measures how much time is spent on the content
- Determines the **topics we are most interested in** → proposes similar
- Applies to **any format**, not just video (carousels with long text = "long-form explainers" included)
- **Trigger**: text-dense carousels, long videos with value, formats that require careful reading

### Shares
- Directly affects reach: more shares = more relevant to the target
- Bonus: whoever receives the content through a share contributes to visibility and conversion
- **Trigger**: memes, counter-intuitive takes, identity-laden content you "have to show off too"

### What No Longer Counts
- **Likes**: users are less and less inclined to give them → devalued driver
- **Followers**: profiles with Reels at hundreds of thousands of views but only a few thousand followers exist because distribution is topic-centric, not profile-centric
- **Classic time on profile**: out of the creator's control

## 2. Performance Diagnostic Matrix — "lots of X but few Y"

A framework to diagnose content from the numbers and figure out what to fix:

### LOTS OF SAVES, FEW LIKES
- **Meaning**: the audience finds you useful but considers the save the "real" action
- **Fix**: ask explicitly for likes / CTA before the halfway mark / insert a goal to reach

### LOTS OF VIEWS, FEW FOLLOWERS
- **Meaning**: the content works but does not build a relationship
- **Fix**: consistent visual identity elements / a repeated distinctive format / clear CTA

### LOTS OF FOLLOWERS, FEW INTERACTIONS
- **Meaning**: cold audience or one that isn't engaged
- **Fix**: more personal and direct content / recycle past high-interaction posts / talk about the target, not yourself

### LOTS OF FOLLOWERS, FEW BIO CLICKS
- **Meaning**: positioning is clear but does not drive action
- **Fix**: a strong reason to click / talk about the product WITHOUT talking about it / commercial messages at the end of the content

### LOTS OF LIKES, FEW COMMENTS
- **Meaning**: the content resonates but does not polarize
- **Fix**: topics where the target already has an opinion / polarizing questions / ask for personal experiences

### LOTS OF PROFILE VISITS, FEW FOLLOWERS
- **Meaning**: you reach new people but the profile does not convince in 3 seconds
- **Fix**: clear bio / coherent feed / visible CTA / vertical topics for the target

### LOTS OF SAVES, FEW SHARES
- **Meaning**: useful content but not "socially shareable"
- **Fix**: emotional lever / identity-laden topics / situations the target faces often

## 3. Repost Strategy (feature launched August 7, 2025)

### Why it exists
The algorithm favors **discovery (non-followers)** over loyalty → your community sees you less and less → following someone today almost excludes them from your feed.

### What a repost is
You redistribute one of your pieces of content in the feed as a **new post** (not a Story, not a link), shown to YOUR followers. It partially bypasses topic-centric distribution.

### Best practice
- **Repost within 3 hours** of publishing → the first hours decide distribution
- **Publishing time** = when the audience is online → use insights / analytics
- Diagram: `Post → Repost → Your Followers`
- The repost influences distribution in the following hours (cascade effect)

## 4. Hook Patterns — 8 Non-Inflated Hooks

Replace clichéd formulas with more specific framing:

| # | NO (cliché) | YES (effective hook) | Why |
|---|-------------|----------------------|-----|
| 1 | "Tips for X" | "Everyone tells you to X, but no one explains HOW" | Makes them shout "it's true!" + promises a solution |
| 2 | "How to X even if Y" | "Can you really X even with Y in so little time?" | Curiosity + a specific challenge |
| 3 | "X for those with Y" | "If you are [group X] and never want to [problem Y] again, watch this" | One-to-one + a real problem |
| 4 | "My method for X" | "If tomorrow I woke up with [big problem] and wanted [Y] within [time], here's what I'd do" | Realistic + a measurable goal |
| 5 | "How [bragging] for Y" | "Here's exactly what you have to do for X if you want Y" | Action → result, no vagueness |
| 6 | "How I grew X" | "In [time] I took X from [A] to [B]" | Numbers + empathetic transformation |
| 7 | "What I learned after Y years" | "It took me [forever] to learn it, I'll teach it to you in less than [very short time]" | Time contrast + authority |
| 8 | "X tips for Y" | "If you are going through [moment Y], these are the X actions to avoid ending up with [outcome Z] within [time T]" | Hits whoever is living the problem + consequences |

## 5. Why Organic Matters in the ADV Era (5 Reasons)

When the client says "organic content is useless, just sponsor and that's it":

1. **Experimentation** — test formats in organic → replicate the ones that work in ADS (saves time + money)
2. **Community** — sponsored brings new people, organic builds loyalty and consolidates trust
3. **Values + USP** — organic = channel to share values, differentiate from competitors
4. **Coherence** — whoever sees the ADV checks the profile. Last post from 2021 = they run
5. **Social listening** — knowing / listening to the audience = you understand what will work in ADS before spending on it

## 6. Recognizable Brand Patterns (lesson from case studies)

Big brands that dominate social respect 3 levers:

### Strategic use of trends
Adapt every trend to your **tone of voice + community**, do not copy. Trend = vehicle, identity = substance.

### Mascot / Visual identity
Give a recognizable face. A mascot cannot be replaced by AI or copies. Substitute with your face if you do not have a mascot.

### Active social listening
Know the audience + what they want + what they expect. Reply to comments with **ironic / coherent personality** (not generic responses).

## 7. Eras / Key Periods

### 2018-2019: Growth Hacking & Bot Era
- Follow / unfollow tactics, bots, automation
- Dominant community growth hacking
- Theme: the algorithm was manipulable through mechanical volume
- The decline of mechanical tactics begins

### 2020-2021: Pandemic + Reels Launch Era
- **August 2020**: Reels launch as the response to TikTok
- Reels = privileged visibility on Explore
- "Share also to feed" option introduced
- Shift toward short videos
- Organic growth still accessible for those who adopt early

### 2022-2023: Saturation + Carousel Format Era
- Twitter-style carousels take off as a format
- The market saturates: "DM me info" stops working
- Auto-subtitled talking videos everywhere (a format that will die later)
- Audience sophistication begins

### 2024: Distribution-by-Interest Era
- The algorithm openly shifts toward interest-based
- Try-and-Real launch (testing on new audiences)
- Growth decoupled from follower count

### 2025: Transition Era
- **January 2025**: Meta drops fact-checking → Community Notes (following X / Musk). Risk of fake news.
- USA vs TikTok (January 2025): ban threat → RedNote (Xiaohongshu) as an alternative
- **August 7, 2025**: Repost feature launch
- Social fragmentation by country: "the future will not be universal"
- Instagram filters removed (January 2025)

### Current: Topic-Centric Era
- **3 pillar metrics consolidated**: Saves, Watch time, Shares
- Likes + followers definitively devalued
- Profile-centric → topic-centric
- Carousel + audio enters the Reels tab = format revival
- Long-form explainers work (high watch time)
- Repost becomes a survival strategy

## 8. Documented Historical Anti-Patterns
- **Follow / unfollow** (2018-2019) → soft ban
- **Engagement bots** → recurring ban waves
- **Chasing every new feature** with no strategy → creator burnout
- **Like-bait / comment-bait** → devalued as a signal
- **"DM me info"** (2022-2023) → saturated, does not convert
- **Static talking head** (2024+) → dead without dynamism
- **Only organic with no ADS / Only ADS with no organic** → both suboptimal

---

## 9. Legal Notes on Content (best practice)
- **Memes**: usable in organic and ADV with caution (verify copyright)
- **Viral hooks in reels**: usable
- **Movie / TV show clips**: legal risk, weigh carefully
- **Viral photos / videos**: not automatically free — many trends born abroad are not compatible with EU / IT regulation
