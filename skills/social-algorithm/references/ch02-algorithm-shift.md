# Chapter 2: The Epochal Algorithm Shift

## Core Idea
Instagram has changed structurally. It is not a trend, it is not a new feature: it is a mutation in the way the platform distributes content. Anyone who does not update their strategy already has obsolete content.

---

## 1. What Worked BEFORE (the old model)

- **Follower-based distribution**: more followers = more guaranteed visibility
- **Linear path**: discovery → follow → community → sale
- **Community as the launch pad**: your content was shown first to followers as a test, then distributed to non-followers based on the initial metrics
- **The goal was reaching non-followers** after validating with the community
- **Small accounts struggled**: very low organic distribution compared to TikTok / YouTube

---

## 2. The 2 Structural Changes

### Change 1: Distribution by Interest (technical, depends on the platform)
- IG shows content the platform **believes is of interest to you**, not what you follow
- Example: you get married → IG pushes wedding content. Your interest shifts → wedding content disappears from your home feed, even though you still follow the wedding creators
- **Implication**: you no longer have any guarantee that the people who follow you see your content
- Follower count is **no longer directly proportional to sales** (it never fully was, but now really not)

### Change 2: Passive / Dopamine-Driven User (human, depends on us)
- **BEFORE**: you opened Instagram with intent (see friends, learn, get informed)
- **TODAY**: you open it out of habit, with no intent. The experience is dopaminergic: you scroll looking for stimuli that trigger you
- IG becomes like **television**: passive, you consume without real engagement
- True for almost every platform (partial exception: YouTube)
- Slot machine effect (TikTok-style)

> Distribution by interest + passive users go hand in hand. Chicken or egg.

---

## 3. The 3 Tangible Consequences on the Profile

### Consequence 1: Lower Average Interactions + Higher Variance
- **Engagement drop of -20/24%** compared to the past
- A passive user is harder to engage
- **High variance**: a few top performers + a few flops + the **middle band disappears**
- When content takes off, it really takes off. When it doesn't, it stays on your low average
- Example: average of 3-4k views. When a piece breaks through → 8-15k. Almost nothing in between.

### Consequence 2: Loyalty / Nurturing Is Less Effective
- **Time on profile** = a devalued KPI (you no longer control how many / when your followers see your content)
- Before: those who followed you consumed X pieces of content → loyalty → purchase
- Today: someone who follows you may never see you → no organic nurturing
- **You compensate elsewhere**: Stories, free resources, email flows

### Consequence 3: The Algorithm Rewards Content More
- Good content **takes off regardless of profile size** (like TikTok)
- It lets even those starting from zero grow without being influencers
- **Double-edged**:
  - PRO: easier to reach new people
  - CON: harder to build community + colder audience
- Confirmation: the **Try-and-Real** feature was created SPECIFICALLY to push content to new audiences

> Quick test: go to the insights of your latest posts. You will see that 20-30% distribution to non-followers is constant, regardless of whether the piece performed well or poorly.

---

## 4. Implications for Content Creation

### The Fundamental Mistake to Avoid
Making content as if you were talking to your community. Almost all content reaches a NEW audience.

### Before You Could Say
"Don't worry if this piece isn't catchy: your community sees it anyway, it just goes deeper." → OBSOLETE.

### Today
If the content is boring / too deep / unintelligible to someone who doesn't know you → **no one sees it**.
It doesn't go to the community → only followers interact. It hits empty space.

**Every piece of content has to work for someone who is seeing you for the first time.**

### Competitive Density — You Compete With Everyone, Not 3 Rivals
Under topic-centric distribution your content is shown next to **everyone** who covers that interest, not just the handful of accounts a viewer chose to follow. As long as a person is interested in the topic, they are bombarded with content on it. So the bar is no longer "better than the 3 competitors my followers also see" — it is "does this stand out among *all* content on the topic." Two practical levers:
- **Contrarian opening**: start with a line the viewer does not expect — dismantle a common myth — instead of the generic "X tips for Y."
- **Visual differentiation**: vary the look so your videos don't read as one more interchangeable talking head. Sameness gets skipped.

> The element that makes you stand out is **you** — your experience, your vision, *how* you say it. It does not require more time, more editing, or more budget: it requires a different mindset. If ChatGPT could write the content, you are not recognizable.

### Directional Content > Chasing Views and Trends
Because the platform pushes to new people, the tempting move is "make viral content to reach everyone." Resist it. Distribution is interest-based, so content is shown most to people **in the purchase window / inside the interest bubble** — the ones actually looking for your solution. Chase volume and you build an account with reach you can't convert (loyalty is already harder). Stay **directional**: speak to the person you want to help, even on wide topics.
- **Exception — mass markets**: if the target is enormous (weight loss, B2C "make money", dropshipping), volume IS the play and chasing reach is justified. Everywhere else, prioritize direction over views.

---

## 5. The 3 Key Elements in Post-Shift Content

### Element 1: Recognizability + Association with Your Domain
Every piece of content is an opportunity to remind people of 2 things:
- **Who you are**
- **What you do**

**Practical rule**: do NOT make content too far from your domain (unless you work in a mass market). Wide topics but INSIDE your bubble. Otherwise random audiences arrive and don't understand what you do.

**Insight**: people no longer follow for information (AI gives better info). They follow for **alignment**.

### Element 2: Complementary Resources (occupying off-platform space)
- A 60-second piece → attach a resource → drive to email list / channel
- Move traffic from ephemeral content to something that makes people **remember** you
- NOT every piece has to have "comment X to receive the guide"
- **Right frequency + on the right content**
- The Anglo market has been doing this for months, even large creators work with resources + newsletter

### Element 3: Don't Take Anything for Granted
Every piece of content suitable for someone seeing you for the first time.
- Explain context
- Don't assume prior knowledge
- Stay accessible even on deep topics

---

## 6. Alignment vs Information — The New Growth Lever

A counterintuitive point for growth: **be totally yourself**.

Every piece of content has to contain:
- A piece of you
- Your experience
- Your vision
- Your way of doing things
- Your style, your character

The more **personalized** the content, the more it works — not because it goes viral, but because the viewer says "I'm aligned with this person, I want to follow them".

Information is a **commodity** (AI). What is not a commodity is who you are.

> It is no longer "what you say", it is "how you do it and how the content is handled"

---

## 7. Capturing Attention vs Getting Views

- Reaching people + getting views = **easy**
- What is hard is turning those views into **something else** (followers, leads, clients)
- To do that you have to **capture attention** = leave an impression
- Recognizability + personalization + resources = the tools to occupy a small space in the potential client's mind

---

## 8. Stories — The Last Outpost for the Relationship

Stories are now **fundamental**, not optional, because:
- **Guaranteed 24h distribution** to followers
- The only certainty that the people who follow you are seeing you
- The real community has moved from feed content → Stories

### Common Stories Mistakes
- "Do I have to post Stories every day?" → no, you have to post them **to communicate something**
- Lack of an **intentional communication plan** (an editorial calendar is not enough)
- Treating them as filler

### How to Use Them Correctly
- Intentional about what you are conveying
- Especially when you talk about yourself as a person, not just what you do
- Show values, life, behind the scenes
- Client cases, stances, technical behind the scenes

---

## 9. Complementary Channels — Stop Single-Platform Dependence

IG is getting closer to the TikTok model = a platform for **traffic and attraction**. Not loyalty.

You need channels to **build loyalty outside the feed**.

### OWNED Channels (you own them)
- **School / Circle**: private communities, free or low-cost. Weekly live Q&A, on-demand materials, they warm up the relationship
- **Newsletter / Email**: a direct contact, an extremely powerful sales channel when treated as such (not as "I send a weekly email telling you something")

### NON-OWNED Channels (other social networks)
| Channel | Pro | When to add |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| YouTube | Click intent + loyalty-building (long-form) | Always, if you have the voice / face for long videos |
| LinkedIn | Different audience (B2B, industry) | If you sell to professionals |
| TikTok | Young audience, content-based ranking | Gen Z target, domain suited to the format |
| Substack | Channel for readers | Only in sectors where the audience reads long content |

### Newsletter — How to Actually Use It
Not just "I send a weekly email telling you something". A newsletter:
1. Is a **powerful sales tool**
2. With a **logical thread** between emails, it becomes a series of content that builds awareness + brings people closer to the service
3. Works without being a "pot seller" — done in a very indirect, human way

---

## 10. Recap of the 2 Changes + Effects

**2 changes**:
1. Distribution by interest (technical)
2. Passive users (human)

**What works LESS**:
- Traditional community building
- Time-on-profile as a metric
- Engagement as a primary goal

**What works MORE**:
- Recognizability + clear domain
- Free resources to take people off-platform
- Stories as the relationship channel
- Complementary channels (newsletter, YouTube, LinkedIn, school)
- Personal alignment (no longer info)

> These updates are **neither good nor bad**. They are just changes in the dynamics. You have to learn to live with them and adjust priorities.

---

## Anti-Patterns
- **Content for the community** assuming they see it → invisible to cold viewers → flop
- **Boring / deep content with no hook** → ends up nowhere
- **Ignoring Stories** → you lose the only direct relationship channel
- **A "I send one email a week telling something" newsletter** → wasting a powerful channel
- **Only Instagram** → fragile, total dependence on a volatile algorithm
- **Chasing vintage engagement rate** → a devalued metric
- **Content far from your domain** → random audience, no scaling
- **Chasing virality** instead of capturing attention

## Key Takeaways
1. Distribution = by interest, not by follower count. Definitive.
2. Passive / dopamine-driven users = every piece of content has to capture in 3 seconds
3. An engagement rate -20% is normal, it is not your fault
4. Every piece of content has to work for someone who doesn't know you
5. Stories = community; Feed = attraction; Email / School = off-platform nurturing
6. At least 1 complementary channel (YT / LinkedIn / Newsletter) on top of IG
7. A recurring lead magnet turns views into a list
8. Recognizability in the first 3 seconds: who you are + what you do
9. Publish only inside your domain to avoid random followers
10. People follow for **alignment**, not information (AI has won the info game)
