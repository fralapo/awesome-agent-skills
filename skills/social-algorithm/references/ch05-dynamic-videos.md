# Chapter 5: Dynamic Instagram Videos — 4 Techniques to Stop Flopping Them

## Core Idea
**Static talking videos are dead.** A talking head in front of a white wall no longer has any effect, unless you (a) are an exceptional communicator, (b) have professional editing, or (c) are already well-known / authoritative. For all the other "mortals", you need 4 levers of dynamism + 1 lever of uniqueness (personal experience) to stand out.

---

## 1. The Old Model That No Longer Works

### What Dominated 2022-2024
- Clips from podcast speeches and various interviews
- Very heavy subtitles / aggressive editing
- Twitter-style carousels
- Short videos with overlay text that said nothing
- Static talking head in front of a white wall

### What Has Changed
- **A lot has shifted in the last 6 months**
- What worked a year ago → now you go unnoticed
- Talking head is dead for anyone who is not already authoritative
- Volume of noise = enormous → you have to stand out with different levers

### The 3 Exceptions (who can still do a static talking head)
1. You are an **exceptional** communicator (1% of people)
2. You have **top-tier** editing (significant cost)
3. You are **already known / authoritative** in the market

For everyone else: dynamism is needed.

---

## 2. Core Concept: Dynamism

### The Principle
It is not the content that has to change radically. It is **how you present it**.

- All else equal in the script → dynamism keeps attention higher
- Without dynamism → people scroll away before the 3-second mark
- The algorithm rewards whoever holds attention = whoever is dynamic

### 4 Operational Levers (combinable)
1. Visual elements + metaphors
2. Camera angle changes + movement
3. Custom B-roll
4. Lean script in trailer style

> Combine **at least 2-3** of these levers per video.

---

## 3. TECHNIQUE 1 — Visual Elements + Metaphors

### What It Is
Using **objects coherent** with what you are saying, or using them to create **visual metaphors**.

### Why It Works
- Seeing a person DO something keeps active attention more easily than pure speech
- Visual metaphors help understand concepts more easily
- The brain processes visual before verbal → higher engagement

### Concrete Examples
- A **nutritionist** doing a discursive reel while **cutting an orange** → a change of setting, a coherent object
- A **relationship coach** explaining a concept with the **metaphor of overflowing glasses** → the abstract made concrete
- A **psychologist** who builds **all their communication** on visual metaphors to explain abstract concepts → signature format

### Critical Use Case: Abstract Niches
For niches without numbers or concrete data (psychology, mental coaching, life coaching, spiritual fields) → visual metaphors are **fundamental**, not optional.

Without metaphors → abstract content → boring → scroll.
With metaphors → abstract made understandable + memorable.

### Ideas to Find Metaphors
- Cooking (chopping, mixing, cooking)
- Domestic objects (glasses, weights, books)
- Nature (trees, flowers, seeds growing)
- Sports (workouts, weight, balance)

---

## 4. TECHNIQUE 2 — Camera Angle Changes + Movement

### What It Is
Changing **position or framing** for each frame of the content.

### Minimal Version (Zero Budget)
- **Tripod still**
- YOU move within the scene
- Same script, but you walk / change position between sentences
- Result: all else equal, dynamism noticeably higher

### Advanced Version
- Change **location** for each frame
- E.g. open space → more backdrops available
- Same script but different frames

### Quick Win: Record Standing (not Seated)
Just this one change:
- Seated behind a desk → static, "boring interview"
- Standing → energy, dynamism, higher attention

> All else equal, getting up makes a significant difference in engagement.

### Anti-Pattern
- Seated behind a desk for 60 seconds, motionless
- Same fixed frame from start to finish
- Hands under the desk (not visible)

---

## 5. TECHNIQUE 3 — Custom B-Roll

### What It Is
"Sacred B-roll" = frames of you doing things (walking, writing, reading, working).

### Absolute Rule
**Never stock library.** B-roll has to be YOURS.

### Why
- Stock B-roll is felt immediately
- Anti-personal → ruins the whole personal brand message
- "The fruit of your experience" → has to be your own material

### Procedure — Single B-Roll Session
1. Take **1h or an afternoon**
2. Choose a coherent location (park, home, coworking, Airbnb)
3. Record **10-30 scenes** of you doing things:
   - Reading
   - Writing
   - Cutting fruit
   - Walking
   - Opening a laptop
   - Putting in headphones
   - Anything coherent with your field

### B-Roll Ideas by Sector
| Sector | Suggested B-Roll |
|--------|------------------|
| Nutrition | Kitchen, gym, supermarket, cutting fruit |
| Digital / marketing | Opening / closing a PC, writing, headphones, screens with data |
| Coaching | Reflective walks, reading, journaling, writing |
| Fitness | Workout, weight, equipment, nutrition |
| Beauty / aesthetics | Skin care, products, hands at work |

### How to Use Them
- **Alternating** B-roll with narration keeps attention much more easily
- **Ideal**: B-roll coherent with what you are saying
- **Acceptable**: B-roll that is not perfectly coherent is still better than:
  - Plain video with CapCut subtitles
  - Just talking in front of a wall

> Better mildly coherent B-roll than no B-roll at all.

## 6. TECHNIQUE 4 — Lean Script (Trailer Format)

### What It Is
Slim the scripts down. **You are not writing a speech, you are writing a trailer.**

### What the Script Has to Be Like
- **NO long paragraphs**
- **Short sentences**
- Frequent **line breaks**
- **Jagged** structure
- Direct and concise

### Visual Example

❌ **Wrong (discursive script)**:
"Today I want to talk to you about a really important topic that affects everyone who makes videos on Instagram and is trying to grow their personal brand, because there are things that..."

✅ **Right (trailer-style script)**:
```
Talking videos are dead.

A lot has shifted in the last 6 months.

If you don't update your content, you go unnoticed.

Here are the 4 techniques to regain visibility.
```

### Mindset
**"I'm making the trailer for a movie, not a TED monologue"**
- Trailer = maximum compression of interest in a very short time
- No linguistic "filler"
- Every sentence has to earn the right to exist

### Hack: Reference Based Scripting
If you have trouble writing like this:
1. Find a reference video you like
2. **Turbo Scribe** (turboscribe.ai) → get the transcript + skeletal structure
3. Study how it is structured (sentence length, error → solution sequence, etc.)
4. **Take the structure, fill it with YOUR content**

Applied example: to make 10-minute YouTube videos without rambling → I took YouTube videos from people I admire, analyzed the skeletal structure, plugged my ideas into that structure.

> You do not copy content, you copy structure. The structure is what makes it work.

## 7. The Secret Ingredient: Personal Experience

### The Problem with Generic Content
If the content:
- Could be made by you **without copying** mine
- Is extremely similar to anyone else's
- Says nothing different from what you find on ChatGPT

→ **Why would I recognize you as a reference or write to you to buy?**

### The Diagnosis
"Extremely informative and objective" content = **burnable** today. AI does it better.

### What to Add
Start from a base piece of content, but inject:
- **Personal experience** (what you lived, did)
- **Real situations** connected to it
- **Concrete examples** of your clients / cases
- **Real data** you obtained
- **Your reflection**: what YOU learned

### The Cardinal Rule
> To be recognized as a reference, you have to give **something extra** compared to what is already findable online.

Communication that is **not basic**, **not informative**. I want to see the fruit:
- Of your years
- Of your skills
- Of your experience

In **every single piece of content**.

### Practical Example
A "How to increase video views" piece:
- ❌ Generalist: "3 classic tips to increase views"
- ✅ Personal: "I started from a result I GOT and I explain what got me there"

Nobody can replicate the second. They can take inspiration, but it will always be unique because it is based on your experience.

## 8. Minimum Recording Setup

To apply all this you don't need pro gear:
- Recent smartphone (the latest iPhone or equivalent)
- Light €6-30 (Amazon)
- Cheap tripod
- Decent background (home / outdoor / Airbnb)

> "My place is small / ugly / I don't want to show it" → go out. Park, coworking, Airbnb. Solutions exist. See ch03 for the details.

## Mental Models

- Think of the video as a **movie trailer**, not a TED monologue
- Tripod still + body moving = the zero-budget trick for dynamism
- If ChatGPT can write your content, you are not recognizable
- Abstract niche? Visual metaphors are your weapon
- B-roll = the matchmaker between content and attention
- Experience = the only differentiator vs AI

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **Talking head white wall + CapCut subtitles** = invisible today
- **Stock library B-roll** → felt instantly, anti-personal
- **Scripts in discursive paragraphs** → attention lost
- **Purely informative content** ("3 tips for X") with no personal experience
- **Copying content** instead of copying structure
- **Waiting for pro gear** → an iPhone + €6 light is enough
- **Seated behind a desk, motionless** → zero energy
- **All frames identical** → algorithm boredom + viewer scrolls
- **Abstract niche with no metaphors** → unreachable content for the audience

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Static → dynamic is priority #1 now
2. 4 free levers: visuals, framing, B-roll, script
3. Combine at least 2-3 levers per video
4. Personal experience makes content **non-clonable**
5. Visual metaphors disarm the handicap of abstract niches
6. Jagged script, not discursive (trailer format)
7. B-roll never stock, always yours (1h recording = 10-30 scenes)
8. Turbo Scribe to extract structure from references
9. Tripod still + you moving = the zero-budget trick
10. Minimum setup: iPhone + €6 light + tripod, nothing more needed
