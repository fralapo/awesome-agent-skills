# The 20 Most Important Rules (entire corpus)

Ordered by leverage. Software-agnostic, durable across tools/trends/styles.

1. **Retention is the only lever; length follows.** Make it as long as it stays good, no longer. Every second must earn the next.
2. **Match the experience the audience came for.** Dial editing intensity on the authenticity↔stimulation axis. Disrupting what they came for is the #1 way to lose them.
3. **The edit can't save a weak idea or script.** Fix idea→script→edit in that order; vet the idea before cutting.
4. **Hook in the first 3–5 seconds, multi-channel.** First frame + audio + delivery + text together; a sharp claim (desire / social-proof / controversy / too-good-to-be-true / problem→rescue) that opens loops without revealing the payoff.
5. **Place the payoff at the END (for original short narratives); never pay off early.** Open hot only when the context is borrowed/known.
6. **Kill dead air; for talking-head, make speech continuous.** Silence is where viewers leave. Cut waste, not story.
7. **Show, don't tell.** Visualize every important line with B-roll / icon / stock / motion graphic.
8. **Less is more.** Simple, well-executed, well-framed beats complex over-creativity. Remove a layer before adding one; effects must complement what's said.
9. **One consistent signature, not more effects.** A fixed identity (theme + locked text palette + framing) beats clutter — the #1 amateur mistake is too many fonts/colors/effects.
10. **Edit in phases:** cut → visual/motion → sound → color/finish. Don't blend them.
11. **Minimize eye travel; never scatter or overload.** Keep face + captions + key graphic on a short central path; anchor text to the face, clear of platform UI.
12. **Captions are mandatory (most watch muted); use them as signal, not wallpaper.** Key lines only, ≤3 words or one-per-beat, emphasis on the payload word.
13. **Premium motion = overshoot + motion-blur + easing + stagger + secondary motion.** Motion blur on every moving element and eased curves are non-negotiable. The same rules govern character/avatar and hand-drawn animation: shape the velocity graph (height = speed), match the easing to intent (in = enter, out = exit, in-out = traverse), and build once → reuse via presets/subject-swap.
14. **Keep focus continuity exact (eyeline match).** Hold the focal point at the same screen position across cuts so the eye never re-hunts.
15. **Motivated movement and entrances only.** Zoom to emphasize a statement, track to follow movement; graphics move in (or a pop is justified by a sound). No motion or effect without a reason.
16. **Sound is ~half the perceived quality; couple it to motion.** Every movement gets a matched sound; isolated SFX pass; risers only before a real payoff (don't cry wolf).
17. **Music is the emotional engine.** Choose it early, map a mood per segment, cut on beats/dips, sync swells to topic shifts, duck under VO; favor loopable, clean-cut, under-used audio.
18. **Grade and compose for perceived quality even when slow.** Grade for the look (a scroll-stopper); rule-of-thirds, one focal element, negative space for text, foreground for depth.
19. **Default to clean cuts; hide cuts with J/L-cuts, transitions, or peak motion.** Fancy transitions usually look worse than a hard cut.
20. **Be distribution-aware.** The platform ranks the video, not the app; tailor to what THIS platform rewards; virality = emotion + timing + shareability — editing amplifies an already-resonant moment.
